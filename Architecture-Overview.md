## 3. SYSTEM FLOW (END-TO-END)  

### **Step 1 — Input Arrives (Wave-State)**  
The system receives a signal, question, pattern, or configuration.  
This is the **1-State** (wave, instability, exploration).

---

### **Step 2 — KRP Processing (Intelligence Layer)**  
The input passes sequentially through:

1. **MeaningRoot (MR)** — What does this mean?  
2. **RouteRoot (RR)** — What paths can exist?  
3. **TrustRoot (TR)** — Which paths are valid & stable?  
4. **TimeRoot (TmR)** — Which valid path resolves fastest?

This reduces the behaviour space from *infinite possibilities* to *one ideal collapse candidate*.

---

### **Step 3 — Collapse Math Evaluation**  
Using:
- stability energy E(x)  
- center-distance D(x)  
- contradiction magnitude C(x)  
- symmetry rules  
- time optimization  

The system identifies the best possible resolution point.

---

### **Step 4 — C-State Activation**  
The collapse engine triggers:

x* = argmin E(x)

This is the universal collapse rule.

The system leaves Wave-state (1) → enters Collapse-state (C).

---

### **Step 5 — Output Reaches Center-State (0)**  
The final output is:

- stable  
- contradiction-free  
- symmetry-aligned  
- minimal-time  
- truth-aligned  

This is the **0-State**, the system’s equilibrium.
